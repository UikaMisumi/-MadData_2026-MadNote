Tianhao Zhou, Yujia Chen, et al. Feb 2026

The Problem

Existing thermal odometry approaches are predominantly geometric and often fail across diverse datasets. Darkness, dust, and smoke severely limit traditional optical sensors.

The Method

We propose TOM-GS, integrating learning-based odometry with Gaussian Splatting (GS) for dense mapping, with dedicated thermal image enhancement.

Thermal infrared sensors, with wavelengths longer than smoke particles, can capture imagery independent of darkness, dust, and smoke. This robustness has made them increasingly valuable for motion estimation and environmental perception in robotics. Existing thermal odometry and mapping approaches, however, are predominantly geometric and often fail across diverse datasets while lacking the ability to produce dense maps...
